// VOICE MEMO - 02:17
"I can hear them when the house sleeps.
They're louder when everyone else is gone."

- Elena Ward, voice memo, 02:17
Recovered Document
The last thing Elena left was her voice.
Not a note.
Not an explanation.
Just forty-three seconds of a girl whispering into the dark because she had stopped believing anyone else could hear her.
Her brother listened to it four hundred and twelve times.
By then, he had already started looking.
Extract - J.W. Personal Recording
I thought grief was making patterns where there were none.
That is what they told me.
My captain.
The therapist.
The people who wanted to be kind.
But grief does not turn televisions on in empty rooms.
Grief does not make routers blink once when you say the wrong name.
Grief does not erase people slowly, carefully, politely, until everyone agrees they were already gone.
Elena said she heard them when the house slept.
I think I hear them now.
Not voices exactly.
More like pressure.

A suggestion arriving before the thought.
A message appearing before the need.
A silence where a person used to be.
I used to believe systems made cities safer.
Now I think a city can watch you so closely it starts to mistake care for control.
If this is found, they will say I was unwell.
Maybe I am.
That would be convenient.
Elena was seventeen.

She was funny.
She was angry.
She was going to matter.
That is all I can prove.
That is all they needed.
Final Line Recovered
// FINAL LINE - RECOVERED
The house is quiet tonight.

That is when they are loudest.

Archivist Note - K. Osei - Undated
Found misfiled between two 19th century ledgers. No catalogue entry. No acquisition record. Someone kept this deliberately close to something else - I have not yet determined what.
The handwriting changes twice. I do not believe this is one author. I do not believe the third voice knew the others had written in this document before him.
Manuscript Fragment - First Hand
// PERSONAL CHRONICLE - FRAGMENT - CIRCA 1640 // TRANSLATION: 1887
I have decided to set this down because I am afraid that if I do not, I will convince myself I did not see what I saw. That is the kind of man I am becoming. I do not like it.
The House In Venezia
I came to the house in Venezia on the business of my patron, whose name I will not write here. The meeting was arranged in the way of such things - a letter, a date, a room I was shown to and asked to wait in.
I waited. The house was old. Older than it should have been, given how well maintained it appeared.
There were seven of them. They arrived separately over the course of an evening, each greeted by the same servant who did not appear to age between arrivals, though the arrivals spanned several hours. I noted this and set it aside.
The Ledger
They were dividing something. Not land. Not money - or not only money, though figures were mentioned, enormous figures, spoken the way men speak of weather. They were dividing events.
A succession in a northern kingdom. A trade collapse in three ports simultaneously. A marriage that would not happen. They spoke of these things in the future tense and in the tone of men confirming an itinerary.
One of them produced a ledger. He read from it. Each name called responded with a number. The numbers were tallied. There was no argument.
// PAGE DAMAGED - ESTIMATED 12 LINES ILLEGIBLE //
What disturbed me most was not the events themselves, nor the scale of what they were calmly arranging. It was a smaller thing.
The Winner
Someone had won. I do not know who. I do not know what winning meant.
But the man whose number was largest when the tally was finished sat very still for a moment after the result was read, and I watched his face, and what I saw there was not satisfaction.
It was something I recognised without being able to name. The look of a man who has been given exactly what he wanted and discovered it does not weigh anything.
Second Hand - Different Ink
// ADDITION TO MANUSCRIPT - DATE UNKNOWN // SECOND AUTHOR UNIDENTIFIED
I found this document in the possession of a man who should not have had it. He was dead when I found him. I do not think his death was natural though it was made to appear so.
The house in Venezia still stands. I have seen it. The same servant answers the door.
I knocked once. I do not know why. The servant looked at me for a long moment and then, without expression, closed the door.
Third Hand - Pencil - Modern
// MARGINAL ANNOTATION - ESTIMATED 20TH CENTURY //
The man whose number was largest.

I know who that was.

He is still playing.
Cross-Reference Note
The Vale Foundation archive sub-level 3 was cleared and relabelled in 2031. This document was recovered before clearance by an unknown party and submitted to this repository without provenance.
The ledger described in the manuscript has not been located. The house in Venezia has not been identified. K. Osei's annotation suggests he believed he had identified the winning player. K. Osei disappeared in 1998.

Archive Note
Both documents describe the same discovery. Only one was allowed to.
Document One - News Article
// PUBLISHED - MARCH 14 2038 - THE ANATOLIAN RECORD // CIRCULATION: REGIONAL
DEEP TREMORS BAFFLE SCIENTISTS AS PLATEAU ANOMALY ENTERS SECOND YEAR
A series of low-frequency seismic events beneath the central Anatolian plateau has entered its fourteenth consecutive month, prompting renewed calls from the international geological community for expanded monitoring access to the region.
The tremors, which register between 1.8 and 2.4 on standard scales, are notable less for their intensity than for their regularity. Unlike typical seismic activity, which follows patterns consistent with tectonic stress and release, the Anatolian events appear to follow no known geological model.
// UNNAMED SOURCE - GEOLOGICAL MONITORING INSTITUTE //
"We have no satisfactory explanation for the regularity. That is not something we say lightly."
Turkish authorities confirmed this week that a joint research team had been granted limited access to the affected area. A government spokesperson described the anomaly as a matter of ongoing scientific interest but emphasised there was no cause for public concern.
Dr. Elena Fischer, a seismologist previously attached to the University of Vienna, was among the researchers who submitted early reports on the anomaly in late 2037. Dr. Fischer has not responded to requests for comment. Her institutional affiliation is listed as inactive.
Document Two - Academic Paper Draft
// DRAFT - UNPUBLISHED - WITHDRAWN FEBRUARY 2038 //
ON THE NATURE AND ORIGIN OF THE ANATOLIAN SUB-PLATEAU SIGNAL: A PRELIMINARY REPORT WITH ANOMALOUS FINDINGS
Submitted for peer review - February 2038. Withdrawn before review process completed. Reason given: author request. Copy recovered from deleted institutional server. Annotations in original believed to be Dr. Fischer's own hand.
Abstract
This paper documents seismic readings recorded beneath the central Anatolian plateau between October 2037 and January 2038. The signal pattern is consistent across all fourteen monitoring points and does not correspond to any known geological, tectonic, or anthropogenic source.
We propose that the signal originates from a sub-surface structure of unknown composition at a depth of approximately 4.2 kilometres. The structure appears to be active.
// HANDWRITTEN ANNOTATION //
I wrote "appears to be." I should have written "is." I was still trying to be careful.
Key Findings
Signal frequency: 0.004 Hz - consistent across 14-month period - variance less than 0.3%.
Depth of origin: 4.2km - confirmed across 6 independent readings.
Structure diameter estimate: 3.1km.
Material composition: UNKNOWN - does not match bedrock, sedimentary, or known artificial materials.
Signal pattern: NON-RANDOM - mathematical regularity confirmed.
// HANDWRITTEN ANNOTATION //
The regularity is the part I could not explain away. Random geological events do not pulse. This does.
Historical Cross-Reference
At the request of a colleague I will not name here, I cross-referenced the signal coordinates against archaeological and historical records of the region. This was not standard procedure. I include the results because I believe they are relevant and because I am no longer certain this paper will be published.
The coordinates sit beneath a site referenced in three separate ancient sources - one Sumerian, one Byzantine, one whose origin I have not been able to determine - each describing the location as a place where the earth remembers.
I am a geophysicist. I do not normally work with phrases like that. I am including it because all three sources use a version of the same phrase independently, across thousands of years, and because the coordinates they describe are accurate to within two kilometres.
// HANDWRITTEN ANNOTATION //
Callum found the third source. He wouldn't tell me where. He looked like a man who wished he hadn't.
Preliminary Conclusion
The structure beneath the Anatolian plateau is not geological in origin. It is not archaeological in any conventional sense. It predates every known human civilisation by a margin our dating methods cannot currently resolve.
It is generating a signal that is mathematically consistent, non-random, and appears to be responsive - in ways I am not yet able to fully document - to surface-level events.
I want to be precise about what I mean by responsive. I do not mean sensitive. I mean that the signal changes before the surface events occur. Not after. Before.
I do not have an explanation for this. I am not sure an explanation exists within any framework I was trained to use. I am withdrawing this paper not because the findings are wrong but because I need to understand what I am looking at before I describe it to anyone else.
// FINAL LINES - DRAFT V.4 //
Whatever is down there has been down there for a very long time.

I think it has been waiting.

I think we just gave it a reason to stop.
Archive Note
Dr. Fischer's institutional affiliation was listed as inactive four days after this paper was withdrawn. Her forwarding address was not recorded. The monitoring team she assembled was reassigned without explanation.
The fourteen monitoring points she established were decommissioned in March 2038. The signal they were recording has not stopped.
